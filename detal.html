Chenille yarn is a special type of yarn made from wool or synthetic fibers, with a surface characterized by small, soft, and fuzzy loops. The surface texture of chenille yarn resembles a string of tiny caterpillar-like fibers, creating a soft and plush feel. The name "chenille" comes from the French word meaning "caterpillar," referring to the caterpillar-like structure of this type of yarn.

Chenille yarn is commonly used to make products such as sweaters, jackets, raincoats, blankets, dresses, footwear, and fashion accessories. Because of its soft and pliable surface, it provides a warm and comfortable feel against the skin. It also has good heat-retaining properties, making it a popular choice for winter fashion.
